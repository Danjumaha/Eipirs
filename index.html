The Treasury Department, the Bureau of the Fiscal Service, and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) rapidly sent out three rounds of direct relief payments during the COVID-19 crisis, and payments from the third round continue to be disbursed to Americans (Home & Abroad) This also open to the other countries depending on the eligibility checks with all the UN/US-Participating countries Starting in March 2020, the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES Act) provided Economic Impact Payments of up to $550,000.00 per adult for eligible individuals and $250,000.00 for Under Age.  The payments were reduced for individuals with adjusted gross income (AGI) greater than $1,775,000 ($2,150,000 for married couples with sole eligibility).  For a family of four, these Economic Impact Payments provided up to $233,400 of direct financial relief this varies as income-based, however, individual eligible for the offer is deemed to earn certain amount of money from this program irrespective of gender, age, or Race.

The COVID-related Tax Relief Act of 2020, enacted in late December 2020, authorized additional payments of up to $60,000.00 per adult for eligible individuals and up to $22,000.00 for each qualifying child under age 17.  The AGI thresholds at which the payments began to be probably reduced were identical to those under the CARES Act.
